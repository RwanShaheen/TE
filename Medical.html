  • Fayuom

City Of Hundred Gates

Description

Fayoum sounds like a wonderful destination for nature lovers and those seeking therapeutic experiences. Wadi El Rayan and Lake Qarun seem like must-visit places with their natural beauty. The Ain Al-Silin area also sounds fascinating, especially with its natural springs that have healing properties. It's great to know that the region is abundant in fruits and vegetables, which gives it a serene atmosphere for visitors to enjoy. The Ain Al-Siliyen tourist area seems like an ideal place to experience the beauty of nature, with its green terraces, waterfalls, old mill, and water springs. It's excellent to hear that the area has hotels, restaurants, and markets for tourists. Overall, Fayoum seems like an excellent place to visit for people who want to explore Egypt's natural beauty and enjoy a tranquil environment.

Qarun Lake?!

Fayoum's Lake Qarun is a major tourist attraction, as one of the natural lakes listed in the world natural heritage. It's a freshwater lake situated in the Western Desert and connected to the Nile River via "Bahr Yusuf," an ancient water channel established by King Amenemhat II in the 19th century BC. Additionally, the industrial lakes of Wadi El-Rayyan offer a unique fragrance and coastal environment that visitors can enjoy.

  • Relax your nerves at Ain Al-Siliyen for 100 per person.
  • Enjoy fresh water at Lake Qarun for 500 per person.
  • Location: Fayoum
  • Location: Qarun Lake

  • Safaga

City Of Healing

Description

Safaga in Egypt is a famous tourist destination for medical tourism, as it has natural hot springs and clean white sandy beaches. Many visitors come to Safaga to enjoy the natural thermal water treatments that are highly effective in treating various diseases. Safaga is also known for its tranquility and relaxation, away from the hustle and bustle of big cities, making it an ideal destination for those seeking to experience relaxation and renewal in a peaceful environment. Therefore, medical tourism in Safaga is considered an ideal choice for those seeking complete luxury and relaxation.

Why safaga?!

The area of Safaga in Egypt is characterized by the warm sun shining throughout the year, making it an ideal destination for therapeutic tourism. The region contains natural thermal waters that are effective in treating various diseases, as well as a dry climate, golden sand, and clear water. Some groups caution against traveling to low-lying areas, while other patients can enjoy the effective climate of Safaga in treating many diseases. The village of Mina Fill in the region is famous for recreational and therapeutic tourism, where visitors can enjoy its unique climate and healthy water that is free of bromine, which can cause skin sensitivity. The village also includes a health center that collaborates with one of the most famous health centers in the world.

  • Try diving in the waters of Safaga for 200 per person.
  • Get a personal assistant in the Black Sands for 150 per person.
  • Location: Safaga

  • Oases

City Of Tranquility

Description

Egypt's seven oases, located in the western and eastern desert, have natural springs containing sulfuric and medicinal waters that provide relief for rheumatic diseases.

Why Siwa?!

Siwa is known for its stunning natural beauty, unique terrain, and vibrant culture. Additionally, it boasts Cleopatra's Bath - a famous landmark and believed to be the swimming pool of the historical icon. The bath's water contains sulfur and therapeutic properties that can help treat rheumatism and skin diseases, making it a popular destination for medical trips in Siwa.

Why Bahariya?!

The Bahariya Oasis region is a unique destination for historical, archaeological, medical, and environmental tourism. It has around 400 mineral and sulfur water springs with proven therapeutic value. The region offers a dry, moderate climate and abundant sunshine throughout the year, making it an ideal retreat for relaxation. It's particularly popular among European tourists seeking hospitalization, thanks to its warm waters of up to 45°C in areas like Oyoun Bir Halfa and Oyoun Al-Kasaa. Meanwhile, the famous Al-Bishmo well combines both hot and cold water sources that flow into one deep rocky stream.

Why Farafra?!

Farafra Oasis is a secluded destination known for its crystal mountains, hot sulfur springs, and natural springs surrounded by palm and olive trees. Its warm weather offers respite to visitors seeking relief from colder climates. The oasis boasts several hot springs with unique chemical properties that outperform sulfurous and mineral springs. These therapeutic hot springs can cure many diseases related to bones, joints, respiratory and skin ailments.

Why Dakhla?!

Dakhla Oasis in Egypt has over 500 water sources, believed to treat skin and psychological diseases. It offers 564 therapeutic sites for medical tourism due to its unique chemical composition containing sulfur and minerals that are effective in treating chronic illnesses.

  • Enjoy a daytime tour to either the Fayoum Oasis or Siwa Oasis for 1500 per person.
  • Two-day trip and a night in the White Desert for 2000 per person.
  • Location: Siwa Oasis
  • Location: Farafra Oasis
  • Location: Bahariya Oasis
  • Location: Dakhla Oasis

  • Aswan

City Of Harmony

Description

Aswan is a famous tourist destination in southern Egypt, known for its luxurious health resorts offering medical tourism services. These resorts provide various natural therapies and water treatments using the warm Nile waters to treat diseases. Additionally, they have clinics equipped with the latest medical technologies and offer special programs for healthcare and nutrition.

Why Elphantine & Isis Island?!

The Island of Elphantine and Isis Island Resort are natural tourist areas for environmental therapy. Sand baths are used for therapeutic purposes from March to October in Elphantine, while Isis Island Resort has a special environmental therapy program for rheumatoid patients that combines sand baths and UV radiation exposure for three consecutive weeks. This therapy has led to significant improvements in disease symptoms, immune system efficiency indicators, blood sedimentation rate, and rheumatoid activity indicators.
In general, environmental therapy can be effective in treating many diseases, and it can be used as an alternative or complementary therapy to traditional medical treatments. The Elphantine and Isis Island areas are characterized by excellent environmental conditions that make them an ideal place for environmental therapy.

  • The best eco-therapy at the Sand Bath for 180 per person.
  • Enjoy the hot waters of the Nile for disease treatment for 300 per person.
  • Location: Island of Elphantine
  • Location: Isis Island

  • Ain Sokhna

Gateway To The Red Sea

Description

Medical tourism in Ain Sukhna is popular due to its natural healing properties, including sand and sulfur springs that have been renowned since ancient times for their therapeutic benefits. These minerals and substances can provide the body with energy, rid it of negative energy, and aid recovery from bone, breathing, and skin diseases. The black sand is especially helpful in treating skin diseases, rheumatoid disease, spine and bone pain. Meanwhile, the sulfuric water is renowned for its heat, which helps to relax the body and eliminate negative energy.

Why Sulfur eyes?!

Ain Sukhna is known for its sulfur springs that form small lakes and waterfalls. These natural therapeutic methods are beneficial for treating chronic diseases, breathing and skin diseases, bone pain, gout, paralysis, neurological diseases, sinuses, asthma, common cold, vulgaris, scabies, psoriasis, allergies, and acne. They also aid in muscle relaxation and eliminating negative energy. There are two springs located at the bottom of Mount Ataka, south of the Gulf of Suez, and in one of the Ain Sukhna hotels. Treatment involves staying in the water for 10-20 minutes periodically at a temperature between 37-39°C to open pores and renew cells. After relaxing in the water, patients can rest for a while before resuming activity. For chronic cases, patients may need to stay in the water for more than half an hour, an average of five times a year. Some precautions are necessary for patients with low blood pressure, while high-blood-pressure patients benefit from the warm water.

Why Salt Cave?!

A salt cave is a modern relaxation method that helps visitors relax through its dimly lit atmosphere and soothing music. The cave is filled with salt, which contains sodium and magnesium elements that aid in healing. Inhaling the iodine in the salt provides multiple benefits to the body. Calcium present in the salt strengthens bones and alleviates bone pain, and steam sessions assist in recovering from respiratory diseases, such as asthma and sinuses. To receive treatment, patients sit on the salt-covered ground and attempt to immerse themselves in the salt. Steam sessions are available for patients with respiratory issues, and a single visit takes about 45 minutes. Patients will notice positive changes after their first visit, including relief from psychological and nervous stress, an increase in energy and vitality, and gradual improvement for orthopedic, respiratory, nervous, or digestive patients.

  • Enjoy the wonderful massage and soothing music at the Salt Cave therapy for a full day for 800.
  • Treat your allergies and rejuvenate your energy at the Sulfuric eyes for 600 per person.
  • Location: Salt Cave
  • Location: Sulfur Eyes

  • Sinai

City Of Pharaohs

Description

Sinai, Egypt is known for its natural and historical attractions that draw visitors from all over the world. One of the most famous of these attractions is Pharaoh's Bath, a group of sulfuric hot springs that flow from Mount Pharaoh in the form of a pool with a capacity of 3000 cubic meters per day at a temperature of 27 degrees Celsius. These waters extend along the beach for about 100 meters and have many health benefits for treating various diseases such as rheumatism, digestive, kidney, liver, and skin problems, as well as being used for cosmetic purposes. The warm sand surrounding the area can also be used for natural therapy. The health resort and tourist village of "Pharaoh's Bath Resort" combines environmental treatment, recreational, and sports tourism, making it an ideal tourist destination for visitors from all over the world.
