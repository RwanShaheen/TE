  • The pyramids of Giza and the Sphinx

Giza Cemetery

Description

The Giza pyramid complex (also called the Giza necropolis) is an archaeological site on the Giza plateau in Greater Cairo, Egypt. The complex includes the Great Pyramid, the Pyramid of Khafre, and the Pyramid of Menkaure along with their associated pyramid complexes and the Sphinx statue. These pyramids were all built during the Fourth Dynasty of the Old Kingdom in ancient Egypt between 2600 and 2500 BC. The site also includes several tombs and the remains of a workers' village.

The Great Pyramid and the Pyramid of Khafre are among the largest pyramids built in ancient Egypt, and they were historically popular as symbols of ancient Egypt in the Western imagination. They became famous in Hellenistic times, when the Great Pyramid was listed by the Greek poet Antipater as one of the Seven Wonders of the World. They are by far the oldest and only surviving ancient wonders of the world.

And the pyramids, according to one of the hypotheses, are royal tombs, each of which bears the name of the king who built it and was buried in it, and the hierarchical construction is a stage in the development of tomb architecture in ancient Egypt. Then it developed to take the form of a step pyramid by the engineer Imhotep, the minister of the pharaoh and King Djoser in the Third Dynasty. This was followed by two attempts by King Senefru, founder of the Fourth Dynasty, to build a complete hierarchical form. However, the backs of the two pyramids are not correct in shape, and they are located in Dahshur, one of them has a flattened base, and the other has a smaller shape, about half the size of the first. The engineer, Hemyuno, the architect of King Khufu, was able to achieve the ideal pyramidal shape, and he built the pyramid of Khufu in Giza on an area of 13 acres, and the pyramids of Khafre and Menkaure followed. The pyramid shape was associated with the ancient Egyptians with the idea of the creation of the universe, and they also believed, according to some of their writings and religious texts, that the pyramid is a means that helps the soul of the deceased reach the sky with the idol Ra, and we can sometimes see the sun's rays among the clouds, which also take the pyramid shape, and it was also among these many means that can help them ascend to heaven.

sphinx:

The Great Sphinx is a statue of a mythical creature with the body of a lion and the head of a human. It was carved from limestone, and it is likely that it was originally covered with a layer of plaster and colored, and traces of the original colors are still visible next to one of its ears. Located on the Giza Plateau on the west bank of the Nile in Giza, Egypt, the Great Sphinx is also the guardian of the plateau. It is the oldest known colossal sculpture, its length is about 73.5 meters, including 15 meters the length of its front feet, its width is 19.3 meters, and its highest height from the surface of the earth is about 20 meters to the top of the head. It is believed that the ancient Egyptians built it during the reign of King Khafra (2558 BC -2532 BC), the builder of the Middle Pyramid in Giza. Sphinx head It is believed that the Sphinx was quarried before King Khafre thought of carving it in the form of a statue, and this statue looks to the east, so the original directions were changed in the last century to match the Sphinx's view

  • Qaitbay Citadel

Mediterranean Bride

Qaitbay Citadel is located at the end of Pharos Island in the far west of Alexandria. It was built in the place of the ancient lighthouse of Alexandria, which was destroyed in the year 702 AH, following the devastating earthquake that occurred during the reign of Sultan Al-Nasir Muhammad bin Qalawun. Sultan Al-Ashraf Abu Al-Nasr Qaitbay began building this castle in the year 882 AH and finished From its construction in the year 884 AH. The reason for his interest in Alexandria was the many direct threats to Egypt from the Ottoman Empire, which threatened the entire Arab region. The Mamluk Sultan Qansuh al-Ghuri took care of the castle, increasing its importance and charging it with weapons.

Description

The main tower in the inner courtyard takes the form of a large square-shaped castle with a side length of 30 meters and a height of 17 meters. The castle consists of three square floors, and in the four corners of the tower there are semi-circular towers that end at the top with prominent balconies. These towers are higher than the main tower, and include openings for shooting arrows on two levels. The first floor is occupied by the castle mosque, which consists of a courtyard, four iwans, and defensive passages that allow soldiers to pass easily during the defense operations of the castle. This mosque had a minaret, but it collapsed recently.

As for the second floor, it contains corridors, halls, and internal rooms. The third floor includes a large room (the seat of Sultan Qaytbay) in which he sits to see the ships throughout the day from Alexandria, covered by a cross vault. There is also an oven on this floor for preparing wheat bread, as well as a mill for grinding grain for the soldiers residing in the castle. Sultan Qansuh al-Ghuri renewed the castle and increased its garrison. This castle was neglected during the Ottoman Caliphate period of Egypt.

This castle was built by Sultan Al-Malik Al-Ashraf Abu Al-Nasr Qaytbay Al-Mahmoudi in the year 882 AH / 1477 AD, in place of the old lighthouse of Alexandria, after he went to the site of the old lighthouse and ordered to build a tower on its old foundation, known later as the Citadel or Tabia of Qaytbay, and the construction was completed two years after the date of Construction, and historical sources indicate that the castle was built with some of the stones of the ancient vanished lighthouse, and not in the same place only, and the lighthouse itself was built with some of the ruins of ancient Pharaonic cities such as Memphis and Thebes

And because Qaitbay Citadel in Alexandria is one of the most important castles on the Mediterranean coast, the sultans and rulers of Egypt have paid attention to it throughout the historical ages. It also contains models, photographs, manuscripts, oil paintings, artifacts, and antiquities from the Stone Age, Pharaonic, Greco-Roman, Coptic, Arabic, Sudanese civilization, and the modern era. The museum site overlooks a natural lake, Ain al-Sira Lake.

  • The Egyptian Museum

Egypt Civilization

Description

The Egyptian Museum is the oldest archaeological museum in the Middle East, and houses the largest collection of ancient Egyptian antiquities in the world. The museum displays a large collection that extends from the pre-dynastic period to the Greek and Roman eras. The architect for the building was chosen through an international competition in 1895, which was the first of its kind, and was won by French architect Marcel Dorgnon. Khedive Abbas Helmy II inaugurated the museum in 1902, and it has become a historical landmark in downtown Cairo, home to some of the finest pieces of ancient Egyptian antiquities.

Museum Library

The library of the museum was established since its opening, and a sum of money was allocated since 1899 to buy books, and the Egyptologist Maspero demanded that a permanent sum be allocated for the purchase of books, and Dacros was appointed as its first curator during the period from 1903 to 1906, Then he was succeeded by several trustees, until Monier, who prepared a comprehensive catalog of the library’s contents until 1926. Then the important shift for the library was when Abdul Mohsen al-Khashab took over the management of the library, and Diaa al-Din Abu Ghazi worked with him, who then took over the library’s secretariat in 1950 and had a role Important in preparing book catalogs, increasing external exchange, and expanding the library so that its current size has two floors, two reading halls, and a print store. The library includes more than 50,000 books and volumes of the rarest books in the field of ancient Egyptian, Greek, Roman and ancient Near East antiquities. In addition to other disciplines, the most important of these books are “The Book of Description of Egypt,” “The Book of Antiquities of Egypt and Nubia,” and “The Book of Lepsius.” The library also contains a rare collection of maps, paintings, and photographs.

Museum collections

  • Prehistoric times: This group includes different types of pottery, decorative tools, hunting tools, and the requirements of daily life, which represent the product of the Egyptian before the knowledge of writing, which settled in many places in Egypt in the north, center, and south of the country.
  • Foundation era: It includes the effects of the first and second dynasties, such as the Salat of Narmer, the statue of Khasekhemwy, and many utensils and tools.
  • The era of the Old Kingdom: It includes a group of artifacts, the most important of which are the statues of Djoser, Khafre, Menkaure, the Sheikh El Balad, the dwarf Seneb, Pii the First, his son Meri An Ra, many coffins, statues of individuals, wall pictures, and the Queen Hetepheres collection.
  • Middle Kingdom era: This group includes many artifacts, the most important of which is the statue of King Mentuheb II, statues of some kings of the 12th dynasty, such as Senusret I, Amenemhat III and others, and many statues of individuals, coffins, ornaments, tools of daily life, and pyramids of some Fayoum pyramids.
  • The era of the New Kingdom: It is the most famous group in the museum, headed by the collection of the little pharaoh Tutankhamun, statues of Hatshepsut, Thutmose III and Ramses II, in addition to war chariots, papyri, ornaments, Akhenaten’s collection, the Israel painting, statues of Amenhotep III and his wife T, a set of amulets and writing and agricultural tools, then a group of royal mummies that are displayed In its own hall, which opened in 1994, until the royal mummies were transferred to the National Museum of Egyptian Civilization in Fustat as part of the royal mummies procession event in 2021.
  • Late ages: The collection includes various artifacts, including the treasures of Tanis made of gold, silver and precious stones, which were found in the tombs of some kings and queens of the 21st and 22nd families in San al-Hajar, in addition to some important statues such as the statue of Amun, Mentumhat, a statue of the goddess Tawert, the painting of karar Canop (Abu Qir), the painting of Ankhi, and a group of Nubian antiquities, some of which were transferred to the Nubian Museum in Aswan.

  • Abu Simbel

Pearl of Aswan

Description

It is an archaeological site in southern Egypt on the western bank of Lake Nasser, and is included in the UNESCO World Heritage List. It contains double temples carved from the mountains in the thirteenth century BC, and it was a permanent monument to King Ramesses II and Queen Nefertari. The temples were completely moved to an artificial hill above the High Dam reservoir in Aswan to protect them from sinking due to the construction of the artificial reservoir. Abu Simbel is one of the most important tourist areas in Egypt.

The Rayyan Valley is characterized by its integrated desert environment, including sand dunes, natural eyes, different vegetation and varied animals, as well as marine fossils. The waterfall area is also considered to be one of the different areas of marine sports. The reserve has 15 species of wild animals, the most important of which are (white deer, Egyptian deer, funk fox, sand fox, wolf) and several falcons.

The great temple in Abu Simbel was built during the reign of Ramesses the Great and construction took about 24 years. It is dedicated to the god Amun, Ra-Harakhty, Ptah and Ramesses. The temple features four colossal statues of the pharaoh, up to 20 meters tall, that adorn the facade of the temple and represent Ramesses II seated on a throne.

There is also the hypostyle hall, which is supported by eight huge columns and features images of battle scenes waged by the ruler in military campaigns. The temple also includes a hall of pillars, which has four corners decorated with beautiful scenes of offerings to the gods. The temple is distinguished by its triangular design, which is followed by most ancient Egyptian temples. The axis of the temple was placed in a special way so that it would be repeated twice a year, on October 22 and February 21, when the sun's rays penetrate the temple and shed light on the statue. Of course, the Abu Simbel Temple is considered one of the most famous temples in ancient Egypt and is located on the banks of the Nile River in southern Egypt, specifically in the city of Abu Simbel, which was known in ancient times as "Yibes", and the temple is considered one of the most important monuments in ancient Egypt.

A major operation was undertaken to save the temple from drowning after the construction of the Aswan Dam in the sixties of the last century, as the temple was moved and installed in another place near its original location, at a distance of about 64 meters, in order to protect it from the high water level of the Nile. The small temple was built for the goddess Hathor, Ramses II and his wife Nefertari, and it was built 100 meters from the temple of Ramses II. The temple contains statues of the king and queen of equal size, and Queen Nefertari allocated an important place in the temple, and the statues included princes and princesses along with their parents.

The temple contains a pillared hall supported by six columns, and statues of the gods Horus, Khnum, Thuth, Isis, Satis, and Tawert. Each temple had its own priest who represented the king in the daily religious rituals, and the expansion of art and science education was necessary for the high priest to become a person qualified to perform this role.

  • Alexandria Library

Beacon of Knowledge

Description

The ancient Library of Alexandria, known as the Royal Library of Alexandria or the Great Library, is the largest library of its time. The library was exposed to many fires and its life ended in the year 48 BC. In 2002 it was rebuilt under the name of the New Library of Alexandria. Historians differed about the person who built the library. Some say that Alexander put it in his planning when building Alexandria, and he is the owner of the idea of building it. Some say that Ptolemy the First built it, and others say that it was founded by Ptolemy II, given that he was the one who completed it. Ptolemy the First was the one who ordered the establishment the library and its organization at his expense, then his successor, Ptolemy II, completed an architectural and thematic plan that expresses the balance of Greek thought and the sciences of the time. There is a difference in the year in which it was established, as there are those who say that it was established in 330 BC, and there are those who say that it was established in 288 BC.

The library in the twenty-first century:

The library was revived in a huge project undertaken by Egypt in partnership with UNESCO, where the library was rebuilt in a location close to the old library.

The library has a large collection of books estimated at 2 million books at the beginning of 2013, selected in Arabic, English and French, as well as a selection of books in other European languages such as German, Italian, Spanish and other rare languages such as Creole, Haitian and Zulu. The current collection includes sources from donors from all over the world in various topics.

Alexandria Library Museums:

  • Archaeological Museum

    • An antiquities museum inside a library, and the museum’s collection includes different eras of Egyptian civilization, starting from the Pharaonic era until the Islamic era, passing through the Greek civilization that came to Egypt with Alexander’s invasion.

  • Manuscript Museum

    • It is one of the academic centers attached to the Library of Alexandria. This museum was established by Republican Decree No. (269) of 2002. Many manuscripts were brought from the Alexandria Municipal Library.

The museum is divided into the following sections:

  • Rare vessels section:

    • What is meant by rare containers are the valuables held in the Library of Alexandria, namely: original manuscripts, rare books, maps, old coins, personal belongings of famous people, precious gifts presented to the library, documents, and so on.

  • Microfilm Department:

    • The content of the department has been enriched with rare collections of manuscripts and documents (about thirty thousand manuscripts and fifty thousand documents).

  • Museum Display Section:

    • This section consists of the museum exhibition hall, which is located on level B1 in the heart of the Library of Alexandria, and displays valuable manuscripts, rare books, and other rare library holdings.

  • Science History Museum:

    • The museum displays the development of science in Egypt over three successive historical periods that make up the main sections of the museum: the Pharaonic section, the Greek section, and the Arabic and Islamic sciences section.

  • Planetarium:

    • The Planetarium Science Center consists of the Planetarium and the Museum of the History of Science which is located inside the inverted pyramid below the Planetarium. The museum was designed as a tribute to the scientists whose work contributed to the dissemination of scientific knowledge. The center also includes an exploration hall located next to the planetarium, where visitors can interact with exhibitions dealing with various scientific topics, especially in physics and astronomy.

    The Planetarium Science Center aims to spread scientific culture, exhibitions and workshops are available to visitors regardless of age and scientific background, thus promoting the concept of science centers being educational tools.

  • The National Museum of Egyptian Civilization

Egyptian Civilization

Description

The National Museum of Egyptian Civilization is a museum located in the city of Fustat, Cairo, on an area of 33.5 acres. The museum accommodates fifty thousand artifacts that tell the stages of the development of Egyptian civilization, in addition to a display of the achievements of the Egyptian man in various fields of life since the dawn of history until the present time.

Location and space

The National Museum is located in the Fustat area in ancient Egypt, near the Babylon Fortress, and overlooks Ain al-Sira Lake. The area of the museum is about 33.5 acres, including 130 thousand square meters of buildings, It is expected to house 50,000 artifacts from various eras of ancient Egypt until contemporary history, and includes a group of warehouses for preserving antiquities equipped with the latest technology.

exhibition halls

The museum's holdings will be displayed in 9 halls, including a major permanent exhibition on the most important achievements of Egyptian civilization, with six thematic exhibitions covering the dawn of civilization, the Nile, writing, state and society, culture, beliefs and ideas,and the exhibition of royal mummies. The museum includes extensive temporary exhibition spaces, An hall and center for education and research, as well as an exhibition related to the development of New Cairo. It will serve as a venue for a variety of events, including film screenings, conferences, lectures and cultural activities and will target local, national and international audiences. The museum will include service, commercial, and entertainment buildings, and a research center for ancient materials science and restoration.

The main hall

The main exhibition hall of the museum contains a number of artifacts that give an integrated idea of the Egyptian civilization from the Pharaonic, Greek, Roman, Coptic, Islamic and modern eras.

The Mummies Hall

The hall gives visitors the atmosphere of the Valley of the Kings. The hall includes 20 royal mummies, including 18 kings and two queens from the seventeenth to the twentieth families. The most important of these mummies is the mummy of King Seqenen Ra, King Thutmose III, Queen Hatshepsut, King Ramesses II, and King Ramesses III.

Archaeological dye

The museum is distinguished by the presence of a grille within the museum’s campus, which was first discovered in 1932, and rediscovered during the excavation process prior to building the museum in 2003-2004. The grille is composed of three rows of double eyes, each with 13 eyes. On the north side, there are 13 single springs supported by a brick wall. The springs were built of small red bricks separated by a 50 cm wide corridor, and each group of eyes was separated by a 20 cm wide brick wall. The eyes are round, 80 cm in diameter and 70 cm in height. On the north side, there are 10 rectangular basins, measuring 70 x 70 cm and 90 cm high, separated by a corridor 65 cm wide. These vats are believed to be used to fix the dye after the dyeing process.
